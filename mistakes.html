Common Mistakes الأخطاء الشائعة

These are the most frequently recurring errors made by IB Arabic B students in exams and written tasks. Recognising them is the first step to eliminating them.

Grammar Mistakes أخطاء نحوية

1. Broken Plural Forms جمع التكسير

  • Arabic has irregular (broken) plurals that must be memorised individually
  • Common error: writing كتابات instead of كتب, or مدينات instead of مدن
  • Fix: When learning a new noun, always learn its plural form at the same time
  • Common broken plurals to know: رجل ← رجال | امرأة ← نساء | ولد ← أولاد | بيت ← بيوت

2. Verb-Subject Agreement تطابق الفعل والفاعل

  • When the verb comes before the subject, it is always singular regardless of the subject's number
  • Correct: ذهب الطلابُ إلى المدرسة (not ذهبوا الطلاب)
  • When the verb follows the subject, it must agree in gender and number
  • Correct: الطلابُ ذهبوا إلى المدرسة

3. Dual and Plural Confusion المثنى والجمع

  • Dual (المثنى) is formed by adding ـان (nominative) or ـين (accusative/genitive)
  • Common error: using جمع where مثنى is required when discussing exactly two items
  • Example: طالبان (two students) not طلاب (which means several students)

4. Case Endings (الإعراب)

  • Nominative (مرفوع): subject of a sentence – ends in ـُ or ـون
  • Accusative (منصوب): direct object or after إنَّ and sisters – ends in ـَ or ـين
  • Genitive (مجرور): after prepositions or in إضافة – ends in ـِ or ـين
  • In written exams, case errors are penalised under Criterion A (Language)

Vocabulary Mistakes أخطاء في المفردات

5. False Friends with MSA and Dialect

  • IB Arabic B uses Modern Standard Arabic (MSA / الفصحى) exclusively in writing
  • Avoid colloquial forms: بدي (dialect) → أريد (MSA); ما في (dialect) → لا يوجد (MSA)
  • Common dialect intrusions: هيك، شو، كيف حالك (acceptable orally but not in written tasks)

6. Overusing the Word كثير

  • Students rely heavily on كثير (many/much) as a general intensifier
  • Richer alternatives: عديد من / متعدد / وفير / غزير / لا يُحصى
  • For degree: جداً → for amount with uncountable nouns: كثيراً → for countable: كثير من

Writing and Structure Mistakes أخطاء في الكتابة

7. Ignoring Text Type Conventions

  • Forgetting the date, greeting, or closing in a letter drops Criterion C marks immediately
  • Writing an article without a headline is a common oversight
  • A speech that never addresses the audience (أيها الحضور) loses marks for convention
  • Fix: Before writing, list the required layout features for the text type and tick them off

8. Writing Outside the Word Limit

  • SL Written Task: 250–400 words. HL Written Task: 450–600 words
  • Writing under the minimum means insufficient development – Criterion B suffers
  • Writing significantly over the maximum means the examiner only marks up to the limit
  • Count words during practice to develop an accurate sense of length

9. Weak Connectives and Repetitive Structure

  • Overusing و (and) to join every sentence produces monotonous writing
  • Use a variety: ثم / بعد ذلك / علاوة على ذلك / على الرغم من / بينما / في حين أن
  • Vary sentence length: mix short emphatic sentences with longer, complex ones

10. Translating Directly from English or L1

  • Direct translation produces unnatural Arabic phrasing
  • Example: "I am very happy" → لا تقل: أنا كثيراً سعيد | قل: أنا سعيد جداً
  • Think in Arabic structures, not in your first language
  • Read authentic Arabic texts daily to internalise natural phrasing
Advice: Keep a personal error log. Every time your teacher corrects a mistake, write it down with the correction. Review it before each written task.

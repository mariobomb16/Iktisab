IB Arabic B Themes موضوعات العربية ب

The IB Arabic B course is organised around five core themes, each with prescribed topics and optional sub-topics.

Theme 1 – Identity الهوية

Exploring personal and cultural identity, language, and belonging.

  • Personal identity: name, nationality, family background, values
  • Language and identity: the role of Arabic in shaping who we are
  • Cultural identity: traditions, customs, heritage, and diaspora
  • Physical identity: appearance, age, ability
  • Online identity: how social media shapes self-image
SLHL Personal WritingDiscussion

Theme 2 – Experiences التجارب

Focusing on the variety of human experiences across contexts and cultures.

  • Daily routines and lifestyle choices
  • Travel, tourism, and cross-cultural encounters
  • Rites of passage: school, graduation, marriage, adulthood
  • Migration and living abroad
  • Memorable events and how they shape character
SLHL Narrative WritingLetters

Theme 3 – Human Ingenuity الإبداع البشري

The impact of human creativity on society and the Arabic-speaking world.

  • Science and technology: inventions, digital life, AI
  • Arts and architecture: classical Arabic literature, calligraphy, music
  • Entertainment, media, and journalism in the Arab world
  • Innovation and entrepreneurship
  • Transport, infrastructure, and urban development
SLHL ReportsArticles

Theme 4 – Social Organisation التنظيم الاجتماعي

How communities, institutions, and systems operate across the Arab world.

  • Family structures: nuclear, extended, changing roles of men and women
  • Education systems: public vs. private, challenges and opportunities
  • Government and civic life: rights, responsibilities, law
  • Work and economics: employment, unemployment, labour migration
  • Religion and its role in public life
SLHL EssaysSpeeches

Theme 5 – Sharing the Planet تقاسم الكوكب

Global issues affecting the Arab world and humanity as a whole.

  • Environment and climate: desertification, water scarcity, pollution
  • Health: global pandemics, access to healthcare, mental health
  • Conflict, peace, and international cooperation
  • Poverty, inequality, and humanitarian challenges
  • Sustainable development goals and the Arab region
SLHL Formal TextsDebates
Exam tip: Every written task and oral activity in Arabic B connects to one of these five themes. When practising, always ask yourself: which theme does this text belong to, and what vocabulary field is expected?

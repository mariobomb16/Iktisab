Exam Strategy استراتيجية الامتحان

A structured approach to both exam papers will help you manage your time, avoid common pitfalls, and maximise marks across all assessment criteria.

Before You Write – Choosing Your Tasks

  • Read all task options before committing to one
  • Choose the task where you have the most vocabulary and ideas – not just the easiest text type
  • Check the theme and topic: can you write 300–400 words with genuine development?
  • If two tasks seem equally manageable, choose the one with a more familiar text type

Planning Your Response (5 minutes)

  • Write 4–6 bullet points listing the main ideas you will include
  • Note the text type conventions you must include (e.g. greeting, headline, signature)
  • Decide on the register: formal or informal? First or third person?
  • Think of 2–3 specific examples, statistics, or references you can use
  • A plan takes 5 minutes but saves 15 minutes of unstructured writing

Writing for Criterion A – Language Marks

  • Aim for variety: do not repeat the same vocabulary word more than twice
  • Use at least 3 different tenses across your response where natural
  • Include complex structures: relative clauses (الذي / التي), conditionals (لو / إذا), cause and effect (لأن / بسبب / لذلك)
  • Avoid very simple sentences; every paragraph should contain at least one complex sentence
  • Read your writing aloud mentally – does it sound natural in Arabic?

Writing for Criterion B – Message Marks

  • Address every part of the task prompt – do not ignore any element
  • Each paragraph should have one clear idea with development and an example
  • Stay on topic: every paragraph should link back to the task
  • Avoid generic statements that could apply to any task – be specific
  • Count your words: aim for the middle of the range (300–350 for SL; 500–550 for HL)

Paper 2 – Reading and Listening ورقة الامتحان الثانية

Reading Comprehension Strategy

  • Read the questions before reading the text – you will know what to look for
  • For True/False/Not Given: 'Not Given' means the information is absent – the text neither confirms nor denies it
  • For short answers: answer in your own words; copying chunks from the text suggests you haven't understood
  • For gap fill: always check grammatical agreement (gender, case, tense) of your answer with the surrounding text
  • For matching: read all options before matching any – options may be designed to confuse

Listening Comprehension Strategy

  • Use the reading time before each recording to read the questions carefully
  • Do not write full sentences during listening – use abbreviations and keywords
  • The recording is played twice: use the first listen for gist and the second for detail
  • Pay attention to negation (لا / لم / لن / ليس) – a negated statement often appears in True/False tasks
  • Speakers may paraphrase – listen for meaning, not just exact words from the question

General Exam Advice نصائح عامة

The final 10 minutes of Paper 1

  • Check that all text type conventions are present (greeting, headline, etc.)
  • Check verb-subject agreement and tense consistency
  • Check that your writing is within the word limit
  • Replace any repeated vocabulary with synonyms
  • Do not rewrite large sections – make targeted corrections only

Long-Term Revision Strategy

  • Read Arabic news articles (Al Jazeera, BBC Arabic, Asharq Al-Awsat) for 15 minutes daily
  • Write one timed practice task per week under exam conditions
  • Review flashcard vocabulary by theme, not alphabetically
  • Record yourself speaking for 3 minutes on a theme topic and listen back
  • Build a bank of 10 model phrases per text type that you can adapt
Most important rule: The examiner cannot award marks for work that is not there. A short but well-structured, linguistically varied response will always outscore a long but repetitive one.

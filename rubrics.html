Assessment Rubrics معايير التقييم

Understanding the IB marking criteria for Arabic B is the most direct path to improving your score. Below is a clear breakdown of each assessment component.

Paper 1 – Productive Skills (Written Tasks) ورقة الامتحان الأولى

Paper 1 assesses your ability to write in Arabic across two tasks. Each task is marked out of 25.

Criterion A – Language (10 marks)

MarksDescriptor
9–10Wide range of vocabulary and complex structures; errors are rare and do not affect communication
7–8Good range of vocabulary; structures are mostly accurate; minor errors do not impede understanding
5–6Adequate vocabulary and some variety in structures; errors are present but meaning is generally clear
3–4Limited vocabulary; frequent errors sometimes impede communication
1–2Very limited language; errors frequently impede communication
0Work does not reach a standard described by the descriptors above

Criterion B – Message (10 marks)

  • Does the response address the task prompt fully and relevantly?
  • Is the content developed with appropriate detail and examples?
  • Is the register (formal/informal) appropriate to the text type and audience?
  • Is the response the correct length? (SL: 250–400 words; HL: 450–600 words)
  • Is the argument or narrative logical and coherent?
A high-scoring message is focused, detailed, and stays on topic throughout. Avoid padding or repeating the same idea in different words.

Criterion C – Conceptual Understanding (5 marks)

  • Does the text follow the conventions of the chosen text type?
  • Are layout features used correctly (e.g. date, greeting, headline, signature)?
  • Is the audience addressed appropriately?
  • Does the purpose of the text type come through clearly?
Letters: greeting + closing Articles: headline + byline Speeches: address audience

Note: The IB does not publish exact band descriptors publicly. The descriptions above are based on standard IB Language B marking principles. Always refer to your teacher and the official Subject Guide for the authoritative criteria.

Individual Oral التقييم الشفهي الفردي

The Individual Oral (IO) is your Internal Assessment. It takes place with your teacher and is recorded. At SL it counts for 25% of your final grade; at HL it counts for 20%.

Format Overview هيكل الامتحان الشفهي

Structure of the Oral

StageDurationDescription
Preparation time15 minutesYou receive the visual stimulus and prepare your presentation. No notes may be taken into the oral itself (SL). HL students may take brief notes.
Part 1 – Presentation3–4 minutesYou speak about the visual stimulus, linking it to one of the five IB themes. No interruptions from the teacher.
Part 2 – Discussion5–6 minutesYour teacher asks follow-up questions about your presentation and broader related topics. You must respond fluently and develop your answers.

Total duration: approximately 8–10 minutes.

The Visual Stimulus المحفز البصري

What is a visual stimulus?

The visual stimulus is an image (photograph, illustration, infographic, or cartoon) provided by the IB. It will relate to one of the five course themes. Your task is to describe, analyse, and discuss the image in Arabic.

How to approach the image

  • Describe what you literally see: people, place, objects, colours, mood
  • Interpret the image: what is happening, what message does it convey?
  • Connect to the theme: which IB theme does this image relate to, and how?
  • Personalise: link the image to your own experience, your culture, or the Arabic-speaking world
  • Give an opinion: what do you think about the issue the image raises?

Useful Arabic Phrases for the Oral عبارات مفيدة

Opening your presentation

  • تُظهر هذه الصورة... – This image shows…
  • في رأيي، تتعلق هذه الصورة بموضوع... – In my opinion, this image relates to the theme of…
  • يمكننا أن نلاحظ في هذه الصورة... – We can observe in this image…

Describing and analysing

  • في المقدمة / في الخلفية – In the foreground / in the background
  • يبدو أن... – It seems that…
  • هذا يدل على أن... – This indicates that…
  • يمكن تفسير ذلك بأن... – This can be interpreted as…

Giving opinions and developing ideas

  • من وجهة نظري... – From my point of view…
  • أعتقد أن... لأن... – I believe that… because…
  • على الرغم من... إلا أن... – Although… nevertheless…
  • إضافةً إلى ذلك... – In addition to that…
  • من ناحية أخرى... – On the other hand…

Responding to teacher questions

  • سؤال جيد، في الواقع... – Good question, actually…
  • لم أفكر في ذلك من قبل، لكن... – I hadn't considered that before, but…
  • أودّ أن أضيف أن... – I would like to add that…

Preparation Tips نصائح للاستعداد

During the 15-minute preparation

  • Spend the first 2 minutes observing the image carefully – do not rush
  • Identify the theme and 2–3 sub-topics you can discuss
  • Plan a logical structure: describe → analyse → connect to theme → opinion
  • Think of specific vocabulary relevant to the theme of the image
  • Prepare 2–3 personal examples or references to the Arab world

During the oral itself

  • Speak at a natural pace – do not rush or memorise a script
  • Use complex sentence structures where possible: subordinate clauses, conditionals
  • If you don't understand a question, ask politely: هل يمكنك إعادة السؤال؟
  • Develop every answer – never give one-word responses
  • Show enthusiasm and cultural awareness
Examiner tip: Criterion C (Interactive Skills) rewards students who keep the conversation flowing naturally. Practise with your teacher by holding regular timed discussions in Arabic on each of the five themes.
